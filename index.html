Organizers

Rodrigo Agerri

Ramon y Cajal Research Fellow at the HiTZ Center - Ixa, University of the Basque Country UPV/EHU, where he leads the Text Analysis Unit. His research is focused on multilingual and crosslingual approaches to Information Extraction, including Argument Mining, using Large Language Models. He regularly publishes and serves as area chair at ACL conferences, as well as on AI and NLP related journals. He has helped to organize several workshops and shared tasks such as SemEval 2022 Task 10, NLP-MisInfo 2023, VaxxStance 2021 and OpeNER at LREC 2014.

Elena Cabrio

elena.cabrio@univ-cotedazur.fr

Full Professor at the University of Côte d’Azur and member of the Inria-I3S research team Wimmics. In 2021 she was awarded with a Chair in Artificial Intelligence at the Interdisciplinary Institute for Artificial Intelligence 3IA Côte d’Azur on “AI and Natural Language”. Her main research interests are in NLP, mainly Argumentation Mining, Information Extraction and Hate Speech detection. She is currently coordinating the ANTIDOTE (ArgumeNtaTIon-Driven explainable artificial intelligence fOr digiTal mEdicine) project (CHIST-ERA XAI 2019)

Marcin Lewinski

m.lewinski@fcsh.unl.pt

Assistant Professor in the ArgLab, NOVA Institute of Philosophy and the Department of Communication, NOVA University Lisbon. His research applying philosophical concepts to the study of public argumentation has been published in journals, edited volumes, and special issues. His most recent work is a monograph, Argumentation in Complex Communication: Managing Disagreement in a Polylogue}published by the Cambridge University Press (2023). He has recently led an EU-funded project COST Action European network for argumentation and public policy analysis

Bernardo Magnini

magnini@fbk.eu

Senior researcher at Fondazione Bruno Kessler in Trento, Italy, where he is responsible of the Natural Language Processing group. His research interests cover various topics in NLP, particularly dialogue systems, information extraction, semantic inferences and linguistic resources. He has been General Chair of ACL 2022 in Dublin.

Marie-Francine Moens

sien.moens@kuleuven.be

Professor Emerita of the Department of Computer Science at KU Leuven, Belgium. She is holder of the ERC Advanced Grant CALCULUS (2018-2024) awarded by the European Research Council. She is currently the director of the LIIR research lab specialized in natural language processing, multi-modal processing and machine learning at KU Leuven. She is associate editor of the journal IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence. She is a fellow of the European Laboratory for Learning and Intelligent Systems

Serena Villata

villata@i3s.unice.fr

Research director (DR) in computer science at the CNRS and she pursues her research at the I3S laboratory. Her current work focuses on artificial argumentation, with a specific focus on legal and medical texts, political debates and social network harmful content. She is the author of more than 150 scientific publications in AI. Since July 2019, she has been awarded with a Chair in AI on “Artificial Argumentation for Humans”. Since 2019, she is a member of the French Committee for Digital Ethics
